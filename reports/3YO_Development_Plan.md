## 🧪 ACCESSORIES RECOMMENDATION

**Current status: ALL three have ZERO accessories applied.**

### What Each Accessory Does (from HRP Help):
| Accessory | Effect | Best For | Cost |
|-----------|--------|----------|------|
| **Blinkers** | Narrows field of vision, improves focus | Horses that run wide or lose concentration mid-race | Apply/Remove anytime (free?) |
| **Shadow Roll** | Prevents seeing own shadow, steadies nervous horses | Nervous or head-shy horses | Apply/Remove anytime |
| **Lasix** | Medication for bleeders; can improve consistency & desire in the stretch | Horses who fade in the stretch or have inconsistent runs | Apply/Remove anytime |
| **Bute** | Helps aching joints, slightly slows aging | Older horses with leg issues | Apply/Remove anytime |
| **Gelding** | May improve speed and consistency (diminishes with age) | Colts that aren't breeding candidates | ⚠️ **IRREVERSIBLE** |

### My Recommendations:

| Horse | Blinkers | Shadow Roll | Lasix | Bute | Gelding |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------|------|---------|
| **Caros Compass** | ❓ Test first race without, add if he runs wide | ❌ No indication needed | ❓ Try after 2nd race if he fades in stretch | ❌ VG soundness, doesn't need | ❌ Wait — breeding value unknown |
| **Golden Shuvee** | ❌ Ideal forehand suggests natural focus | ❌ No indication | ❓ Consider later | ❌ Ideal soundness | ❌ Wait — best prospect |

> **Bottom line:** Start all three **without any accessories** for their first race. This gives us a clean baseline. If they show behavioral issues (running wide, fading, head-bobbing), we add accessories one at a time to isolate the effect.
