# Training

## Overview
Training is a meter management tool only — it has no impact on horse performance beyond meters. There are 6 training options. You can train once per day at a track or farm, and the stamina meter must be above zero to train.

---

## CORRECTION: No "Light Train" Option Exists

Topic: Light Train Does Not Exist
Claim: There is NO "Light Train" option in HRP. The official help page lists exactly 6 training options: Standard, Std-Short, Std-Long, Heavy, Hvy-Short, and Hvy-Long. Previous documentation incorrectly listed "Light Train" and "Rest" as options — these do not exist.
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx (Section 21)
Snippet: "There are 6 training options available which are Standard, Std-Short, Std-Long, Heavy, Hvy-Short, and Hvy-Long."
Confidence: High

## Official Meter Movement Ranges

Topic: Training Meter Movement - Standard Variants
Claim: Standard training options provide the following meter changes:
| Train Type | Condition | Stamina | Distance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard Short | 10-14 Gain | 7-11 Loss | 0-2 Short |
| Standard | 10-14 Gain | 8-12 Loss | 0 |
| Standard Long | 10-14 Gain | 9-13 Loss | 0-2 Long |
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx (Section 26 - Meter Movement)

## Short vs Long Stamina Differences

Topic: Short/Long Stamina Trade-off
Claim: Short train variants cost slightly less stamina than their standard counterpart; Long variants cost slightly more. This is true for both Standard and Heavy tiers.
- Std-Short (7-11 Loss) vs Standard (8-12 Loss) vs Std-Long (9-13 Loss)
- Hvy-Short (24-30 Loss) vs Heavy (27-33 Loss) vs Hvy-Long (30-36 Loss)
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx (Section 21)
Snippet: "Std-Short will move stamina down slightly less than Standard on average and Std-Long will move stamina down slightly more than Standard on average."
Confidence: High
