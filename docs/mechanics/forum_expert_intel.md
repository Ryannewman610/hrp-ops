# Forum Expert Intel — Training & Racing Strategy

> Extracted from 1,264 General/Breeding threads + 439 Ask The Experts pages.
> Insights from veteran HRP players.

## Race Frequency & Rest Cycles

- **Race every 4-6 weeks** as a general rule. Every 2 weeks is "super aggressive"
- **Post-race recovery:** 10 days rest after a race, then 2 works in the next 14 days → 90% ready
- **Monthly pattern:** 2-5 works per month at normal distances, race once per month
- **Three weeks rest** before a big race (e.g., stakes at Santa Anita) is common prep

- **Stakes-level recovery:** 4-5 weeks before a major race (confirmed by ATE Thread 28131)

## Virgin Work Benchmarks (3f)

- **Top tier:** 34.65b-34.68b on Jan 1 farm works
- **Stakes winner range:** 35.50b-36.00b — most stakes winners fall here
- **Average:** "above average" starts around 34.78
- Many players wait until **5f works in February** to evaluate

## Weight & Age Dynamics

- When 3-year-olds turn 4, they **lose their weight advantage** (under race conditions)
- It takes "a few months" for the development curve to catch up
- Veterans reap benefits until ~March, then 4-year-olds catch up

## Gelding (Speed Mechanic — ATE Thread 28333)

- **Geld early** if no breeding plans — "ungelded colts don't win the big races most of the time"
- "Some horses may show **significant speed improvements** after being gelded"
- "It is quite possible that some horses may **never reach full speed potential** without being gelded"
- A lot of horses **do NOT need gelding** — it's horse-specific
- Decision: check if horse is plateauing on speed → try gelding

## Statebred Strategy (💰 ATM Machine)

- Win a **SB MSW** and a **SB NON-WINNERS** race → retain ALL open allowance conditions
- Effectively doubles earning opportunities: statebred + open track

## Claiming & Starter Strategy

- Starter races require horse to have **started for claiming price X or less since [date]**
- Match starter price to horse's past claiming prices (10k, 25k, 50k)
- "50k starter non winners of 2 or 3 life" is the premium format

## Turf Specialist Threshold

- True turf specialists run **at least 1 full second faster** on turf than dirt
- Not many true specialists exist — most are "turf OK" rather than specialist

## Conformation Impact

- **Ideal/Very Good** soundness ratings correlate with racing longevity
- Back Leg Soundness and Front Leg Soundness are the most critical
- Horses with Poor soundness have shorter careers
- Confo is important but **pedigree + on-track performance** are the 3 pillars of breeding

## Maiden Race Intelligence

- **SB wins don't count** toward allowance conditions ("Have not won N races other than maiden, claiming, starter, or **statebred**")
- MSW (maiden special weight) is preferred over MCL (no claim risk)
- Progressive ladder: MSW → NW1 → NW2 → NW3 → NW4 → Stakes
