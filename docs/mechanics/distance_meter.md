# Distance Meter Mechanics

## Overview
The distance meter ranges from 5S (short) to 5L (long). It is meant to be a way to shorten or lengthen a horse's best distances.

---

## How the Distance Meter Affects Performance

Topic: Distance Meter Performance Impact
Claim: The distance meter can shift a horse's best distances by about 1 furlong (max ~2 furlongs). When shifted in one direction, the other direction is weakened. Example: a horse that runs best at 6f gets a +5L, it may now prefer 7f but won't run 5f as well because 5f is now farther from its shifted best distance.
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx
Snippet: "The distance meter is capable of impacting a horse's best distances by about 1 furlong. The max amount it will impact a horse's best distance will be about 2 furlongs. When the horse is impacted in one direction it will take away from the other direction."
Confidence: High

## Drift Toward Zero During Maintenance

Topic: Distance Meter Maintenance Drift
Claim: During nightly maintenance the distance meter drifts back toward a zero neutral position.
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx
Snippet: "During nightly maintenance the distance meter will have a tendency to move back to a zero neutral position."
Confidence: High

## How Training Moves the Distance Meter

Topic: Training Distance Meter Movement
Claim: Training moves the distance meter as follows:
- Standard-Short (Std-Short): 0-2 Short
- Standard: 0 (no movement)
- Standard-Long (Std-Long): 0-2 Long
- Heavy-Short (Hvy-Short): 1-3 Short
- Heavy: 0 (no movement)
- Heavy-Long (Hvy-Long): 1-3 Long
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx
Snippet: "Std-Short train and Hvy-Short train will move the distance meter towards short. Std-Long train and Hvy-Long train will move the distance meter towards long."
Confidence: High

---

## How Works Move the Distance Meter

Topic: Works Distance Meter Movement
Claim: Works move the distance meter based on distance:
- 2f works (Breezing): 0-2 Short | (Handily): 1-3 Short
- 2.5f works (Breezing): 0-1 Short | (Handily): 0-2 Short
- 3f to 5f works: 0 (no movement)
- 5.5f+ works (Breezing): 0-1 Long | (Handily): 0-1 to 0-2 Long (increases with distance)
- 1m+ works (Handily): 0-2 Long
- 1 7/16m+ works (Breezing): 0-2 Long | (Handily): 1-3 Long
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx
Snippet: "2f and 2 1/2f works will move the distance meter towards short. 5 1/2f+ works will move the distance meter towards long."
Confidence: High

---

## Can the Distance Meter Be Actively Managed?

Topic: Active Distance Meter Management
Claim: Yes. The distance meter can be actively managed through training type selection (Short vs Long variants) and by choosing work distances. Short works (2f, 2.5f) push it shorter, long works (5.5f+) push it longer. Standard/Heavy trains without Short/Long have no distance effect. During maintenance it drifts back to zero, so active management must be ongoing.
Source URL: https://www.horseracingpark.com/help/index.aspx
Confidence: High

## SimRacingForm Interpretation

Topic: Distance Meter Practical Guidance
Claim: 1L indicates aptitude for longer races (6f+), while 1S indicates short sprint aptitude (2-3f). The distance meter can be used as a tuning tool to align a horse's meters with the races you want to enter.
Source URL: https://simracingform.com/horse-racing-parkbasic-user-guide/
Confidence: Medium
